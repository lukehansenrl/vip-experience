# RL Clubhouse VIP — Product Brief

The one-pager Claude reads before generating ads. Keep it tight, current, and true. If anything here goes stale, the whole agent drifts.

---

## What it is

A 6-week, 1-on-1 high-ticket coaching program for Rocket League players. Each client gets paired with a pro coach for a personalized rank-up plan, weekly reviews, and access to the RL Clubhouse community of pros and serious players.

## The promise

**"Rank up in 6 weeks. Or we coach you free until you do."**

The guarantee is the headline. It is real and it is the differentiator. Most coaches won't make it because most coaches can't deliver it. This program can because the coaches are actual pros and the plan is personalized to the player's exact blockers.

## The offer

- **Price:** $497 (founding-member tier; first call collects payment) — **NEVER PUBLISH THIS PRICE IN ANY AD CREATIVE**. See "Price secrecy rule" below.
- **Format:** 1-on-1 with a pro, personalized 6-week plan, weekly sessions, community access
- **Capacity:** Hard cap of 60 active clients at any given time (currently 47/60 filled)
- **Sale mechanic:** Application form → qualified prospects book a 45-min call via Calendly → payment collected on the call
- **Guarantee:** Free continued coaching until the player ranks up

## Price secrecy rule (HARD RULE)

The price ($497) is **never published on a public ad surface**. Not in headlines, not in primary text, not on a static image, not in video captions, not on a landing-page screenshot we might post. Reasons:

1. Screenshot risk: a Facebook ad showing "$497" can be screenshotted to Reddit, Discord, or X within hours and become the dominant narrative ("$500 to play a video game?"), undermining the funnel before any context is delivered.
2. Anchoring risk: leading with price collapses the perceived value of the program before the VSL has had a chance to set the frame.
3. Filter logic: the form already handles budget filtering. The ad's job is to attract the right person, not to pre-disqualify them with sticker shock.

Also avoid **price-proxy language** that creates the same exposure: "high-ticket", "$500-level coaching", "premium investment", "exclusive coaching program". If a hook only works by hinting at the price, it doesn't work for us.

What we *do* lead with: the guarantee, the rank-up promise, the pro-player access, the frustration narrative, the scarcity ("13 founding spots left").

## Who it's for (ICP)

See `icp.md` for full detail. Short version:

- Adult (23+, or 18+ with high disposable income from a full-time job)
- Currently ranked **Plat to Champ** in Rocket League
- Has a full-time job or otherwise earns enough to afford $500 on a first call
- Identifies as "serious about improving" — willing to commit 6 weeks of focused work
- Frustrated by being stuck at their current rank despite playing a lot

## Who it's NOT for

Critical because the ads need to repel as much as attract — every disqualified lead costs us a sales call.

- Under 18 (legally and practically — they can't pay)
- Bronze/Silver/Gold (program is built for Plat-Champ ceiling-breakers; lower ranks need fundamentals coaching, not pro-level work)
- Casual players ("I just want to have fun")
- Anyone whose stated budget for improving is under $500/year
- People who want a one-off session — this is a 6-week relationship

## Proof we lean on

- 12+ video and text testimonials with specific rank progressions (e.g., "Champ 2 | 1020 → 1136 MMR in 13 days")
- Pro-player coaches (real RLCS-tier names, not random Champ-1 freelancers)
- Existing 60-client roster as a "this is selling" signal
- The guarantee itself — nobody offers it because nobody can back it up

## Core objections

What kills the sale and what the ad copy needs to pre-empt:

1. **"$500 is a lot for a video game."** — Reframe as a 6-week career-style investment vs paying for one-off sessions forever.
2. **"I've tried coaching before and it didn't work."** — That's why we built the guarantee. If we don't rank you up, you don't pay more.
3. **"I'm not good enough to be coached by a pro."** — Wrong direction. Pros are the only ones who can see what's actually holding back a Plat-Champ.
4. **"How is this different from a Fiverr coach?"** — Personalized 6-week plan + community + pro-tier coaches + the guarantee. One-off sessions don't compound.

## Voice & tone

See `branding.json` for full voice spec. Short version: direct, confident, no fluff, no "unlock your potential" coaching-grift language. We talk to grown men about a real investment in a thing they care about. Wry humor okay. Hype-cringe not okay.

## What success looks like

- Booked call (Calendly confirmation) = the primary conversion event for Meta optimization
- Form submit = the secondary event (cheaper signal, optimize on it until we have 50+ booked calls/week)
- Closed $500 sale = the real metric — track in our dashboard, not in Meta

## Pages in the funnel

- `/vip` — the main paid-traffic destination. VSL (15s gate) → 11-question application form → Calendly embed
- `/onboarding/unqualified` — disqualified destination (rank/budget/age don't fit)
- `/onboarding/qualified` — kept for organic flow (paid traffic goes through /vip end-to-end)

## What changes — and when to update this file

- New testimonials or rank-progression proof points
